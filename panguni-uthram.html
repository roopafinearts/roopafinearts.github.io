Panguni Uthram

Panguni Peru Vizha is a 10 day festival celebrated at the Mylapore Sri Kapaleeshwarar Temple. The highlight of this festival ist he Arupathi Moovar Vizha, where the 63 Nayanmars are taken out in procession, and deities from all the temples in Chennai come to Mylapore to participate in the function.

In our display, at the top, we have the Utsavamurthy - Somaskandar. Murthy means form, and Utsav means festival - so Utsavamurthy refers to the main form depicted in the festival. Somaskanda or Somaskanda Murthy is one of the main forms of God Shiva popular in South India, depicted as Shiva, Uma (Parvathi, the wife of Shiva), their son Skanda (also known as Kumara or Murugan) - all shown on the same pedestal.

The next section of the display shows the Adhigara Nandhi. Every temple of Shiva has an image of Nandhi, the reclining bull and the vahana (vehicle) of Lord Shiva. Adhikara Nandhi (also Nandikeshwara) is a form of Nandhi that resembles Shiva - Adhikara means "authority".

The final portion of the display is the 63 Nayanmars. The Nayanmars were a group of 63 Hindu saints who lived between the 6th and 8th centuries and who were devoted to Lord Shiva. They do not belong to one community; they hail from high and low, rich and poor, and across ages and sexes.
